# DreamLayer — Full-System Audit (2026‑07‑14)

> A five‑dimension, privacy‑weighted audit of the entire DreamLayer stack:
> the Python engine, the on‑glass Lua client, the phone app, the Mac Brain,
> the Rust reality‑core, the registry, and the web surfaces. Grades are
> A–F with +/–; **Overall weights Privacy Integrity at 2×** because it is
> the core product promise. Every finding is grounded in `file:line`.
>
> Method: 42 independent auditors read their assigned surface in full and
> graded against a world‑class bar; four adversarial "spine" investigators
> traced the highest‑risk cross‑module paths. Load‑bearing findings were
> re‑verified by hand against the code before landing here.

---

## AUDIT SECTION 0 — Executive Summary

**Codebase health / GPA.** Weighted across all 34 graded modules the codebase
earns a **B (3.0 GPA)**. The per‑dimension picture is the story: Code Quality
**3.34 (B+)**, Architecture **3.22 (B+)**, Test Coverage **3.05 (B)**,
Production Readiness **2.92 (B–)**, and — tellingly — **Privacy Integrity is the
*weakest* dimension at 2.86 (B–)**. For a product whose entire thesis is "privacy
is the architecture," the dimension the marketing leans on hardest is the one the
code delivers least reliably. That inversion is the headline.

**The three spine findings (the pre‑audit reasoning pass).**

1. **The hidden architectural risk — the privacy contract is enforced by
   *convention at ~89 call sites*, not by a structural choke‑point.** "One gate
   honored everywhere" is, in the code, ~89 hand‑written `allow_capture()` /
   `allow_recall()` guards across **36 modules in ~18 distinct idioms**, each lens
   owning its own `self._privacy` reference. Worse, the default is **fail‑open**:
   four independent copy‑pasted `_AlwaysOn` classes and a second `if self._privacy
   is not None and not …` idiom both mean *a lens wired without a gate captures
   unconditionally*. A surface‑level reviewer sees veil checks everywhere and
   concludes the contract holds; the real risk is that a *new or refactored* path
   that simply forgets the guard leaks silently, with no compile‑time or
   structural backstop.

2. **The highest‑leverage refactor — collapse those 89 guards into one injected,
   fail‑closed `PrivacyGate` + a `@requires_capture` / `@requires_recall`
   decorator.** It touches the most modules (36), lands squarely on the
   2×‑weighted dimension, and converts "one gate honored everywhere" from a naming
   convention into an enforced invariant. It beats the other candidates — the
   12‑mixin orchestrator god‑object (one subsystem, maintainability only), the four
   hand‑synced figment interpreters (multi‑runtime by necessity, already parity‑
   tested), and the memory embedder/vector‑store ladder (one subsystem).

3. **The privacy‑contract vulnerability — Incognito does not actually stop cloud
   egress.** The hub's Incognito shield only disables the hub's *own* cloud tier.
   A paired Mac mini Brain runs its **own** cloud escalation gated solely by the
   Brain's local config, and the hub never tells it about the session. During
   Incognito a Juno question travels hub → `RemoteKnowledgeBrain` → Mac mini
   `/dreamlayer/brain/ask` → `Brain.ask` → `_ask_cloud` → the cloud provider —
   exactly what Incognito promises can never happen.

**The single most urgent fix.** Spine‑finding #3, promoted to **CRITICAL**:
**make the wearer's session posture authoritative across the hub↔Brain seam.**
`set_incognito()` (`orchestrator/ops_brain_switches.py:59`) forces the *local*
router's cloud tier off but sends nothing to the paired Brain; `ask_brain`
deliberately gates on `allow_recall()` (true during Incognito), so the remote
tier — which is `is_cloud=False` and thus never touched by the cloud switch —
launders the query to cloud on the far side (`ai_brain/server/server.py:772‑802`,
`incognito_now()` at `:763` is the Brain's *own* posture, blind to the hub).
Fix: carry a per‑request `no_cloud` flag on every `RemoteKnowledgeBrain` POST and
have `Brain.ask()` honor it. This is the difference between a demo and a promise.

**The single biggest surprise (unexpectedly good).** The **Reality Compiler / on‑
glass safety core is close to bank‑grade, and it *proves* it.** Figments are
provably data‑only (pure dataclasses, `str.replace` + a `{slot:\w+}` regex, a
final byte clamp — no `eval`/`exec`/`compile`/`callable` anywhere in `v2/`); the
deploy boundary enforces signature → revocation → budget in order; and the safety
caps are lifted into pure PEP‑316 functions that **CrossHair symbolically proves
over Z3 on the real call path**, backed by **mutmut mutation ceilings** (survivors
= 0 on `contracts.py`/`signer.py`). The Rust `reality-core` even *strengthens* the
data‑only contract by pre‑tokenizing templates so host text can't smuggle tokens
back in, and drives the *real* Python and JS interpreters bit‑for‑bit in a
differential harness. This is engineering most shipping products never reach.

**Trajectory & debt velocity.** Trending **toward** production quality, but
unevenly, and the debt is a specific and diagnosable kind: **excellent machinery
that isn't switched on, or is guarded by convention instead of structure.** The
plugin system has a real TUF/Uptane trust chain and a subprocess jail — but ships
with `trusted_keys=None`, so a self‑signed key buys in‑process trust. The health
ledger is a clean observability primitive — wired at ~7% of degradation sites. The
capture pipeline fails *closed* — but the Dream‑Mode camera path skips the gate
entirely. mypy is CI‑enforced — over 4 of 323 files. The hard parts exist and are
well built; the remaining work is *wiring, defaulting, and centralizing*, not new
invention. Left unaddressed, the fail‑open veil convention (finding #1) is the
debt most likely to compound into a shipped privacy incident.

**Quick Win** — `openai_api_key: str = field(default_factory=…, repr=False)` and add `__post_init__` bounds checks (`0≤confidence≤1`, `intervals>0`): privacy hygiene + robustness in one edit.

- **BLE/pairing:** LAN pairing correctly absent (web is not a LAN node). BLE exists only in the playground (standard Nordic UART with correct user‑gesture pairing) but is a naive dev client: **no MTU chunking** (multi‑byte Lua exceeds the 20‑byte ATT MTU and truncates), unauthenticated, no reconnection.
- **Quick Win** — Wrap each `loadManifest` in `Promise.allSettled` so one missing manifest degrades a single act rather than killing all motion; and scope/reject raw‑REPL input at the glasses' BLE endpoint so the playground can't be a device‑side capability escape.

### The Honest Verdict

If this shipped to 10,000 users tomorrow, **the first thing to break would be the
privacy promise, quietly, for the subset who did exactly what the product tells
them to do** — enable Cloud on their Mac Brain, then flip Incognito for a private
moment. Their query would reach the cloud anyway (CRITICAL #1), and nothing in the
UI would say so. Close behind, anyone who wired an OpenAI key for cloud reach and
then turned the Cloud *switch* off would still be streaming raw camera frames and
transcripts to OpenAI through Dream Mode and the ingest pipeline (CRITICALs #2–3).
None of these throw errors; they fail silently, which is the worst way for a
privacy product to fail.

**What would embarrass the team** is the gap between the story and the wiring. The
README's auditable‑privacy claim is *mostly* honest and occasionally exemplary —
but a reader who greps, as the README invites, finds the Veil enforced by ~89
copy‑pasted guards with a fail‑open default, a plugin trust chain shipped with an
empty key list so "signed" means "self‑signed," a rate limiter that defends
nothing, a hardware deploy path that awaits nothing, and mypy guarding 4 of 323
files behind a `py.typed` marker that promises full typing. The machinery is real
and often excellent; it just isn't switched on.

**What users would trust that they shouldn't:** the Truth Lens "deception" gauge,
whose micro‑expression channel — a third of its weight for people you know — is
literally random noise; the "erase everything" button, which (via a separate
server endpoint) may not reach the orchestrator's main memory store and leaves REM
consolidation fingerprints of forgotten memories behind; and the 2,689‑test green
checkmark, which in a bare CI environment silently skips the CrossHair proofs, the
differential fuzzing, and the mutation ceilings that make it a real safety net
rather than a comforting number.

And yet — the same audit that finds these holes also finds a provably data‑only
figment interpreter verified by symbolic proof and mutation testing, a
capture pipeline that fails *closed*, a cross‑wearer mesh that puts only an
HMAC‑authenticated feeling on the wire, and a memory‑forget path that scrubs
answer bytes to zero and proves it at the raw‑byte level. The bones are
genuinely good. This is a **B** codebase with an **A‑** core and a **B‑**
privacy‑enforcement layer that its own thesis demands be an A. The distance
between those two grades is the whole job — and, encouragingly, it is
wiring and centralization, not invention.
